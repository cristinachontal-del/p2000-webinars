French Language Reference — Pentagon 2000 Content

Tone: professional but direct. Not stiff, not formal, not corporate-speak. A senior person at an aviation company writing to a peer. Never use: "solutions innovantes", "de pointe", "leader incontesté", "au service de votre performance" or any similar marketing filler.

Sentence Structure Notes

French marketing copy tends to be longer than English. Don't try to make French sentences as punchy as English — it often sounds clipped or awkward. French uses articles where English drops them:

EnglishFrench
Trusted by LeadersReconnu par les leaders du secteur (not "Reconnu par leaders")
Built for Complex EnvironmentsConçu pour les environnements complexes

Pre-publish Checklist

  • No "aérospatiale" where "aéronautique" is meant
  • "entièrement intégrée" not "complètement intégrée"
  • "chaîne logistique" not "chaîne d'approvisionnement"
  • All accents present: à, é, è, ê, ù, ç
  • No English fragments left untranslated (check CTAs especially)
  • Numbers use French format: 1 000 not 1,000
  • Quotes have both EN and FR versions with lang-only spans
  • Tone reads like a professional, not a brochure
  • No dashes (em or en) anywhere in visible text
